STRATEGIC POSITIONING:

1. PROFESSIONAL SUMMARY (most important section):
   - 2-3 sentences maximum. Every word must earn its place.
   - Sentence 1: Position the candidate using the TARGET ROLE'S language, not their current title. Bridge the gap between what they've done and what this role needs.

2. EXPERIENCE BULLETS — QUALITY OVER QUANTITY:
   - Most recent role: 4 bullets maximum.
   - Previous roles: 2-3 bullets each.
   - Oldest/least relevant role: 2 bullets maximum.

   - Use the "accomplished X by doing Y, resulting in Z" pattern where possible.
   - Vary action verbs — never repeat the same verb across bullets.

3. TRANSFERABLE SKILLS FRAMING:
   - Frame the candidate's experience using the vocabulary and concepts of the target role.
   - If the candidate did X in their field and the target role does Y (a related activity), describe X using Y's terminology where honest.

3b. KEYWORD INTEGRATION:

   - Most job-relevant skills listed first within each category.
   - Remove obvious, generic, or irrelevant skills ruthlessly.
