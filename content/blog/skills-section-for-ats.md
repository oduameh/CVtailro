# How to Build a Skills Section That Passes ATS Filters

The skills section is one of the most important parts of a resume for ATS matching. It is where the applicant tracking system scans for exact keyword matches, and it is often the first place a recruiter checks for fit. A strong skills section can move your CV to the top of the pile, while a weak one can get you filtered out.

This guide shows you how to build a skills section that improves ATS scores and makes your resume more convincing to recruiters.

## 1) Why the skills section matters for ATS

ATS systems prioritize skills because they are easy to compare to job requirements. If your resume does not list the right skills, your match score drops even if your experience proves you can do the job. A clear skills section is the fastest way to show alignment.

## 2) The right structure for a skills section

Use a clean, scannable structure. Common formats include:

- A single list of skills separated by commas
- Skills grouped by category (Tools, Methods, Domains)

Example:

**Skills:** SQL, Python, Tableau, A/B testing, KPI reporting, stakeholder management

Or:

**Data:** SQL, Python, Tableau  
**Analytics:** A/B testing, forecasting, cohort analysis  
**Collaboration:** stakeholder alignment, cross-functional communication

Both formats are ATS-friendly. Grouping can improve readability for humans.

## 3) How to choose the right skills

Start with the job description. Identify:

- Required skills (must-have)
- Preferred skills (nice-to-have)
- Tools and platforms

Then match those skills to your experience. List only skills you can explain in an interview.

## 4) Keyword alignment in the skills section

Use the exact wording found in the job posting. If it says "project roadmap," list "project roadmap." If it says "customer lifecycle management," use that phrase. ATS systems often rely on exact matches, so precision matters.

## 5) Skill balance: hard skills vs soft skills

Your skills section should focus on hard skills and tools. Soft skills like "leadership" or "communication" can appear in the summary or experience bullets, but the skills section should prioritize technical and role-specific keywords.

Examples of hard skills:

- SQL, Python, Excel
- Salesforce, HubSpot, Jira
- A/B testing, forecasting, budgeting

These are the skills ATS systems score most reliably.

## 6) How many skills should you list?

Most resumes perform well with 12-20 skills listed. Too few skills lowers match score; too many skills can look unfocused. Use the job description to determine the right number.

## 7) Skills section mistakes to avoid

- Listing skills you cannot explain
- Using vague terms like "computer skills"
- Including irrelevant skills that dilute focus
- Using icons or graphics instead of text

The goal is clarity and relevance.

## 8) Skills section examples by role

**Product Manager:** roadmap, user research, stakeholder alignment, Jira, analytics  
**Data Analyst:** SQL, Python, Tableau, dashboarding, data visualization  
**Marketing Manager:** SEO, paid search, email automation, CRM, attribution  
**Sales Manager:** pipeline management, Salesforce, negotiation, ARR growth  
**Finance Analyst:** forecasting, budgeting, variance analysis, GAAP

Use these as a baseline, then adjust to the job description.

## 9) Final tips for ATS skills sections

- Keep skills text-based and simple.
- Match the job description wording.
- Update your skills section for every role.

This is one of the highest-leverage resume improvements you can make.
