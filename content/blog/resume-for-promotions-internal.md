# Resume for Internal Promotions: Position Yourself for Growth

Internal promotions require a resume that shows leadership, impact, and readiness for higher responsibility. Even if you already work at the company, you still need a tailored resume that frames your achievements for the new role.

## 1) Start with a promotion-focused summary

Example:

"Operations specialist with 5 years of experience leading process improvement initiatives and mentoring junior team members. Reduced cycle time by 22% and improved SLA compliance across three teams."

## 2) Highlight leadership and initiative

Show evidence of:

- Leading projects beyond your role
- Mentoring or training others
- Driving cross-functional outcomes

## 3) Align keywords with the new role

Use the job description for the promotion role and align your summary and skills to that language.

## 4) Emphasize measurable outcomes

Internal promotion decisions still rely on impact. Quantify your results.

## 5) Final checklist

- Are leadership outcomes visible?
- Are keywords aligned with the target role?
- Is the resume ATS-friendly if required?
