# Follow-Up Email After a Job Application: Templates and Timing

Following up after a job application increases your chances of a response. A concise, professional follow-up reminds the hiring team of your interest and keeps you on their radar. This guide includes templates and a timing strategy you can reuse.

## 1) When to follow up

Wait 5-7 business days after applying. This gives recruiters time to review applications.

## 2) Follow-up email template

Subject: Following up on [Job Title] application

"Hi [Name],

I wanted to follow up on my application for the [Job Title] role. I am excited about the opportunity and believe my experience in [key skills] aligns well with the role. Please let me know if I can provide any additional information.

Thank you for your time,
[Your Name]"

## 3) Customize for the role

Add one line referencing the company or team to show genuine interest.

## 4) Keep it short

The best follow-up emails are short, polite, and specific.

## 5) Final checklist

- Did you wait at least 5 business days?
- Is the email under 100 words?
- Did you mention the role and a key skill?
