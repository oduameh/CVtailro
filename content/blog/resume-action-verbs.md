# Resume Action Verbs That Make Your CV Stronger

Action verbs make your resume more direct, confident, and results-focused. The right verb can turn a weak bullet into a strong achievement statement. This guide provides the best resume action verbs and explains how to use them.

## 1) Why action verbs matter

Action verbs signal ownership and impact. They help recruiters understand what you did and why it mattered.

## 2) High-impact action verbs by category

- **Leadership:** Led, Directed, Mentored, Orchestrated
- **Execution:** Delivered, Implemented, Built, Launched
- **Optimization:** Improved, Streamlined, Automated, Reduced
- **Analysis:** Analyzed, Evaluated, Forecasted, Modeled
- **Collaboration:** Coordinated, Aligned, Partnered, Facilitated

## 3) Replace weak verbs

Replace:

- "Helped" -> "Delivered"
- "Worked on" -> "Implemented"
- "Responsible for" -> "Led"

## 4) Examples of stronger bullets

Weak: "Responsible for weekly reports."  
Strong: "Automated weekly reporting in Excel, reducing manual effort by 30%."

## 5) Avoid overused verbs

If every bullet starts with "Managed," diversify your verbs to show different types of impact.

## 6) Final checklist

- Does each bullet start with a strong action verb?
- Are verbs aligned with outcomes?
- Are metrics included where possible?
