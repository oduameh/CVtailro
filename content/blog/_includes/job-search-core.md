## Job Search Execution Framework (Reusable for Any Role)

Even the best resume performs better when paired with a structured job search system. This section provides a reusable job search framework you can adapt to any role or industry.

### 1) Build a role-focused target list

Create a list of 20-40 target companies and prioritize them by fit. For each company, identify:

- The role you are targeting
- The specific team or business unit
- The skills and tools required

This helps you tailor your resume and cover letter more efficiently.

### 2) Create a resume versioning system

Keep a master resume and create tailored versions for each role. Use a naming convention like:

"Resume_Product_Manager_CompanyName_2026-03-03.pdf"

This makes it easy to track which resume was used for which job application.

### 3) Apply with a follow-up schedule

Apply, then follow up within 5-7 business days. A simple follow-up email improves response rates. Track your follow-up date in a spreadsheet so you do not miss opportunities.

### 4) Build a lightweight networking routine

Networking does not require a huge time investment. Each week:

- Reach out to 3-5 people in your target industry
- Ask for insights, not referrals
- Share your interest and relevant experience

This increases your visibility and can lead to warm introductions.

### 5) Use a weekly metrics review

Track your job search like a project:

- Applications submitted
- Interviews secured
- Responses received
- Follow-ups completed

Review these metrics weekly and adjust your approach. If your response rate is low, your resume may need better keyword alignment.

### 6) Keep a feedback loop

After interviews, document the questions and feedback. Use this to update your resume, cover letter, and interview preparation. This creates continuous improvement.

### 7) Maintain momentum

Job searches can be long. The best results come from consistent effort over time. This framework keeps your process focused and prevents burnout.
