# Cover Letter Words to Avoid (And What to Use Instead)

The words you choose in a cover letter affect how recruiters perceive you. Generic phrases and weak verbs make your application blend in. This guide highlights common words to avoid and provides stronger alternatives.

## 1) Avoid vague phrases

Phrases like "hardworking" or "team player" are overused. Replace them with evidence:

- "Hardworking" -> "Delivered projects ahead of schedule"
- "Team player" -> "Partnered cross-functionally to deliver outcomes"

## 2) Avoid filler sentences

Skip lines that do not add value, like "I believe I am a great fit." Instead, show why you are a fit with outcomes.

## 3) Replace weak verbs

Weak: "Helped with a marketing campaign."  
Strong: "Led a marketing campaign that increased conversions by 18%."

## 4) Use role-specific keywords

Align your cover letter with the job description. This improves ATS matching and recruiter engagement.

## 5) Final checklist

- Are statements backed by metrics?
- Are role keywords included?
- Is the cover letter concise and specific?
