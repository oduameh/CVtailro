# How to Turn Your LinkedIn Profile Into a High-Performing Resume

LinkedIn is a strong foundation, but it is not a resume. A resume must be more focused, ATS-friendly, and tailored to the job description. This guide shows you how to convert your LinkedIn profile into a resume that gets interviews.

## 1) The key differences between LinkedIn and a resume

LinkedIn is broad and networking-oriented. A resume is targeted and outcome-focused. Key differences:

- LinkedIn allows long descriptions; resumes must be concise.
- LinkedIn includes endorsements; resumes highlight quantified achievements.
- LinkedIn may include all roles; resumes should prioritize relevance.

## 2) Start with a tailored resume summary

Use your LinkedIn headline as a starting point, then expand into a resume summary with keywords and metrics.

Example:

LinkedIn headline: "Growth marketer | SEO | Paid Media"

Resume summary: "Growth marketer with 7 years of experience in SEO, paid media, and lifecycle marketing, driving 42% organic traffic growth and reducing CAC by 18%."

## 3) Convert LinkedIn experience into resume bullets

LinkedIn descriptions are often long paragraphs. Convert them into short, measurable bullets using the action-result formula. Include tools and outcomes.

## 4) Optimize your skills section

LinkedIn skills can be a keyword bank. Choose the skills that match the job description and add them to your resume skills section in the same wording.

## 5) Remove irrelevant details

Your LinkedIn profile might include older roles or unrelated positions. Your resume should focus on the most relevant experience. If a role does not support your target job, shorten or remove it.

## 6) Align dates, titles, and keywords

Ensure job titles and dates match between LinkedIn and your resume. Consistency reduces recruiter doubt and improves ATS parsing.

## 7) Final checklist

- Is the resume tailored to the job description?
- Are LinkedIn paragraphs converted into bullets with metrics?
- Is the skills section aligned to the job requirements?

If yes, your LinkedIn-to-resume conversion is complete.
