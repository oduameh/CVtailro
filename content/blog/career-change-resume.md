# Career Change Resume: How to Highlight Transferable Skills

Changing careers is challenging, but a well-structured resume can bridge the gap. The key is to highlight transferable skills, align your resume keywords with the new role, and show evidence of relevant outcomes. This guide explains how to build a career change resume that gets interviews.

## 1) Start with a targeted resume summary

Your summary should make the career change feel logical. Example:

"Operations manager transitioning into project management with 8 years of experience in process optimization, stakeholder alignment, and cross-functional delivery. Led initiatives that reduced cycle time by 22%."

This summary uses keywords for the new role while connecting past outcomes.

## 2) Translate your experience into the new role language

Use the job description to identify keywords, then map your experience to those terms. If the role requires "project roadmap," emphasize planning and execution. If it requires "customer insights," highlight your experience with feedback analysis.

## 3) Add a skills section that supports the transition

Include skills that match the target role:

- Tools
- Methods
- Certifications

If you lack a required skill, consider a short course or certification.

## 4) Use projects and training to fill gaps

If you are missing direct experience, add projects, volunteer work, or coursework. Highlight outcomes as you would in a job role.

## 5) Avoid common career change mistakes

- Using a generic resume summary
- Focusing on irrelevant tasks
- Ignoring the new role's language

## 6) Final checklist

- Does the summary align with the target role?
- Are transferable skills clearly highlighted?
- Are keywords aligned with the job description?

If yes, your career change resume is ready.
