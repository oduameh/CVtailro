# Achievements vs Responsibilities on a Resume: What Recruiters Want

Recruiters value achievements more than responsibilities. Responsibilities show what you were supposed to do; achievements show what you delivered. This guide explains how to write achievement-focused resume bullets.

## 1) The difference between responsibility and achievement

Responsibility: "Managed weekly reports."  
Achievement: "Automated weekly reports, reducing manual effort by 30%."

## 2) Convert responsibilities into achievements

Ask:

- What result did this work produce?
- What metric improved?
- What tool or method did you use?

## 3) Achievement-focused bullet formula

"Action + method + outcome + metric"

## 4) Examples

Weak: "Responsible for customer onboarding."  
Strong: "Redesigned customer onboarding and improved completion rate from 68% to 82%."

## 5) Final checklist

- Does each bullet show impact?
- Are metrics included?
- Do bullets align with job keywords?
