# Portfolio and Resume: How to Combine Them for Stronger Applications

A portfolio adds proof to your resume. When your CV includes a portfolio link and project highlights, recruiters can quickly validate your skills. This guide shows how to integrate your portfolio with your resume in a way that is ATS-friendly and recruiter-friendly.

## 1) When a portfolio is essential

Portfolios are especially important for:

- Designers and UX roles
- Developers and engineers
- Marketing and content roles
- Product managers and analysts

If your work is visual or project-based, a portfolio can be a major advantage.

## 2) Where to include your portfolio

Add a portfolio link in the header near your contact information. Use a clean URL. Avoid long tracking links that look unprofessional.

## 3) Align portfolio projects with your resume

Your resume should reference the same projects in the portfolio. Use consistent names and metrics to avoid confusion.

## 4) Portfolio summary section

Add a short "Selected Projects" section with 2-3 highlights and outcomes. This gives recruiters a reason to click.

## 5) ATS considerations

ATS systems may not follow links, but recruiters do. Keep the link visible and easy to copy.

## 6) Final checklist

- Is the portfolio link visible?
- Are project names consistent between resume and portfolio?
- Are outcomes quantified?
