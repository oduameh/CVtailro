# Certifications on Your Resume: Where They Belong and Why They Matter

Certifications validate your skills and can significantly improve ATS matching. When a job description lists a certification, not having it is often a filter. This guide shows how to place certifications on your resume and maximize their impact.

## 1) When certifications matter most

Certifications are especially important for:

- Cloud and IT roles (AWS, Azure)
- Project management (PMP, Scrum Master)
- Finance (CFA, CPA)
- Healthcare (clinical certifications)

## 2) Where to list certifications

Create a dedicated "Certifications" section near the bottom of your resume. If the certification is highly relevant, you can also mention it in the summary.

## 3) Format certifications clearly

Use the format:

"Certification Name — Issuer — Year"

Example:

"AWS Solutions Architect — Amazon — 2025"

## 4) Include relevant keywords

Use the exact certification name from the job description. This improves ATS matching.

## 5) Final checklist

- Are certifications listed clearly?
- Do they match the job description?
- Are dates included?
