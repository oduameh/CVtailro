# Resume for Internships: How to Stand Out With Limited Experience

Internship resumes are about potential, learning ability, and relevant skills. Employers expect limited experience, but they still want evidence of impact. This guide shows how to build an internship resume that is ATS-friendly and interview-ready.

## 1) Start with a focused summary

"Computer science student with experience in Python, data analysis, and project-based problem solving. Built a dashboard project that reduced reporting time by 25%."

## 2) Emphasize projects and coursework

Use projects to show skills:

- Academic projects
- Personal projects
- Hackathons or competitions

Describe outcomes and tools.

## 3) Highlight transferable skills

Include communication, teamwork, time management, and problem solving, but show them through examples.

## 4) Keep it one page

Internship resumes should be concise and ATS-friendly. Use standard headings and a clean format.

## 5) Final checklist

- Are projects described with outcomes?
- Are skills aligned with the role?
- Is the resume one page and ATS-friendly?
